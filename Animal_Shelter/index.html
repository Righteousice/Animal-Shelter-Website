Cell Dog Program

The Animal Shelter Society, Inc. and local correctional facilities have joined forces to develop a specialized program designated to saving the lives of dogs that have spent an extended period of time at the Shelter.

The dogs selected to participate in this program are given basic training, crate trained, and taught to obey commands. They are ready to be placed into a permanent home once they graduate from the program.

For additional information, please contact the Animal Shelter Society, Inc. at 740-452-1077.

About The Animal Shelter

Since it's founding in 1959, The Animal Shelter's leaders have focused on raising the necessary funds and spending them judiciously to care for the area's abandoned animals. Contributions from so many in this community have also allowed the Shelter to serve the needs of the homeless animals of Muskingum County.

We are committed to housing, caring, and finding homes for the abandoned pets in our community. All of our pets are spayed or neutered before placement into their forever homes. We have established several programs to aid in our quest to find our abandoned pets a permanent home as well as controlling our area's pet overpopulation.

We are a 501(c)3 Non Profit facility relying solely on adoption fees and generous donations for our funding. We receive absolutely NO government funding!

We are an "adoptable no kill" shelter, meaning we never euthanize for space.

Spay and Neuter Program

Our low cost spay/neuter program has reduced the number of abandoned pets in our area and the surrounding counties. We require the fees to be paid prior to scheduling your pet for surgery. Please contact the Animal Shelter for additional information at 740-452-1077.

Volunteer

The Animal Shelter Society, Inc. would not be able to help countless of homeless animals each year without the support of our generous volunteers. Whether it's volunteering at the Shelter or helping with one of our fundraising events, each and every volunteer makes a huge difference in the lives of the animals waiting on their furever homes.

  • Volunteer Jobs
  • Sweep or mop the floors
  • Fold Laundry
  • Wash Dishes
  • Walk dogs
  • Play with the puppies and kittens
  • Give dogs or puppies a bath
  • Change litter pans
  • Fill food and water dishes for cats and dogs
  • Pass out treats

When volunteering at the Shelter

Please close all cages after placing pets in them. Please keep all adult dogs ten feet apart when taking them for walks. Please sanitize your hands between handling each pet.

**Volunteers must fill out an application to volunteer with the Shelter. All volunteers must be a minimum of 16 years of age and a parent/legal guardian must sign liability waivers. Criminal history checks are done for any and all volunteers for our Shelter.
